I believe the best way to learn is by doing. My personal projects often start as simple questions tied to my hobbies, like "what would my movie data look like visualized?", and evolve into practical applications where I can test and grow my skills.

My professional and academic work has been about applying that same hands-on approach in more structured settings. My internship with Google taught me about collaborative coding and industry standards, while my research at Harvard showed me how data is used to tackle complex, large-scale problems.

Beyond the technical work, my experiences as a Course Assistant and with the Asian American Student Association were just as important. They taught me about the human side of any project: clear communication, community building, and the empathy required to explain a complex idea or lead a team effectively.
